Introduction:

What is a Giant Panda?

Giant pandas are memoriable for their black and white coat. They live in high temperate forests in China that are compact with bamboo. Giant Pandas mainly eat the bamboo and depending on what part of it they are eating they have to eat 26-84 pounds of it every day.

Why is this plan needed?

Giant Pandas are a sort of umbrella species that protect other species like multicolored pheasants, the golden monkey, takin, and crested ibis. They also bring economic benfits to China as they help draw in tourists.

Goal Details:

  1. In order to increase the numbers of Giant Pandas researchers and biologists will need to
    make their natural habitat more abundant with bamboo. In addition to this certain laws will
    need to be put into place in order to protect the wellbeing of the pandas. One more thing
    for biologists to do is raise the pandas in captivity where they will be safe.
  2. In order to ensure a safe habitat we must first ensure that no wildlife poachers can
    encroach on the panda’s land. Then we must make sure that there are no predators
    or competitors around for the pandas. Then finally we must make sure that no
    environmental factors pose a threat to them.
  3. In order to make sure that there is enough food we must make sure that the panda’s
    main food source, bamboo, can grow in large quantities. To do that we must make
    sure that the ground it grows on is healthy enough for it’s liking; we must make
    sure the soil is fertile.
